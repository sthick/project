ABOUT ME

I am a current Senior in the School of Information at the University of Michigan following the Information Analysis track with a side focus in User Experience Design. I am interested in new uses for technology in order to integrate business, society, and design to make information more compatible for everyday uses.

Data Visualization and Data Manipulation are two key duties I am entrusted with through my postition as a manager for the University of Michigan Softball. I have worked specifically on the application of pitcher data, hitter exit velocity and am currently creating individual player profiles to be continuously updated and reported to the coaching staff.

During my time as a Cyber Security Engineer Intern with ETAS, a subsidiary of Robert Bosch, I grew my skills in communication, technical applications, and presntation and visualization. Through working in the cyber security division known as escrypty my main focus was working with our company designed application known as “Unicorn” which is contracted out by major automotive companies for testing purposes.

While working for the Cleveland Cavaliers I was entrusted with team and individual projects. These projects ranged from data storage, marketing programs, ticket exchange app creation, and database research.

I have an in depth knowledge of Adobe PhotoShop and Adobe Illustrator that I have been practicing for over 6 years. Projects include: propoganda for Lapeer High School's production of Bye Bye Birdie, tickets for Lapeer High School Graduation, logo creation for Hawkshire Meadows living community, infographic design, and more
